A Volume-of-Fluid method for multicomponent droplet evaporation

This work presents a new simulation method that accurately models how complex liquid droplets evaporate in gas flows, a process critical for industries such as energy, cooling technologies, and advanced manufacturing. By using a Volume-of-Fluid (VoF) approach combined with a novel boundary condition technique, the method captures the transfer of multiple liquid components into the gas phase with high fidelity. Unlike traditional models, it can handle realistic, non-spherical droplet behavior and turbulent environments without sacrificing stability or efficiency. The approach enables better design and optimization of systems involving fuel sprays, cooling sprays, or chemical processes where multicomponent evaporation plays a key role.
