Fully Resolved Direct Numerical Simulations of Buoyancy-Driven Bubbly Flow in a Vertical Channel

This study employs interface-resolved direct numerical simulations (DNS) using a conservative diffusive interface (CDI) method to analyze buoyancy-driven bubbly flow in a vertical water channel at a 2.7% air volume fraction. The simulations consider varying numbers of deformable air bubbles with fixed density and viscosity ratios, while systematically varying the Galilei and Eötvös numbers to examine the effects of buoyancy and surface tension forces.

Results show that bubble distribution transitions from near-wall clustering at low Eötvös numbers (less deformable bubbles) to center-channel accumulation at high Eötvös numbers (more deformable bubbles), significantly altering flow patterns. The study further explores turbulence characteristics, energy transfer mechanisms, and bubble size distributions, offering insights into complex interactions with industrial applications.
